# Tiger Woods - Fact File

## Personal Information
- **Full Name:** Eldrick "Tiger" Tont Woods
- **Born:** December 30, 1975, in Cypress, California
- **Height:** 6'1" (185 cm)
- **Turned Professional:** August 1996

## Career Achievements
- **Major Championships Won:** 15 (as of 2023)
  - Masters Tournament: 5 wins (1997, 2002, 2003, 2019, 2022)
  - PGA Championship: 5 wins (1999, 2000, 2006, 2007, 2019)
  - U.S. Open: 3 wins (2000, 2002, 2008)
  - Open Championship: 3 wins (2000, 2005, 2006)
- **PGA Tour Wins:** 82 official tournaments
- **World Golf Rankings:** Spent 683 weeks at number 1 (record holder)

## Notable Records
- Second all-time in major championships (behind Jack Nicklaus who has 18)
- Youngest golfer to win the Masters (age 21 in 1997)
- Won the Masters at age 46 in 2022, defying odds after serious car accident in 2021

## Playing Style
- Known for exceptional ball-striking and course management
- Famous for his mental toughness in competitive situations
- Revolutionized the professional golf fitness standards
- Known for his powerful swing and competitive fire

## Personal Life
- Parents: Kultida (Thai) and Earl Denison Woods
- Married Elin Nordegren (2004-2010), then Erica Herman (2023)
- Has two children: Sam and Charlie
- Involved in various charitable endeavors, including the Tiger Woods Foundation

## Comeback Story
- Took a break from golf (2014-2017) due to health issues and injuries
- Underwent spinal fusion surgery in 2017
- Made a remarkable comeback, winning the 2019 Masters after 11 years without a major championship
- Continued competing despite challenges from a 2021 car accident

## Legacy
- Transformed professional golf globally, increasing its popularity
- Pioneered modern sports training and fitness in golf
- One of the greatest athletes of all time
- Mentored numerous young golfers
